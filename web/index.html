Photo Guide
01 of 07 steps
Open Panel Door Fully
Before taking the photo, make sure the panel door is fully open and nothing is blocking the view — no cables hanging in front, no tools resting on the door.
Critical
02 of 07 steps
Shoot Straight On
Stand directly in front of the panel — no angling to the side, no tilting up or down. A perpendicular view gives the AI the clearest geometry to read.
Accuracy
03 of 07 steps
Good Even Lighting
Avoid dark corners, harsh shadows, or flash glare on plastic covers. Use ambient light or aim a torch at the ceiling above the panel — not directly at it.
Lighting
04 of 07 steps
Entire Panel in Frame
Capture the full panel from top busbar to bottom cable tray — every row of breakers must be visible. Don't crop the top or bottom of the enclosure.
Completeness
05 of 07 steps
Labels Must Be Readable
Get close enough that breaker ratings (32A, 16A…) and type markings (MCB, RCCB…) are clearly legible. The AI reads these to identify every component.
Accuracy
06 of 07 steps
Hold Steady — No Blur
Use both hands or brace your elbows against the wall. Motion blur is the most common cause of failed detection — even slight movement at close range matters.
Sharpness
07 of 07 steps
You're All Set!
Door open, standing straight, good light, full panel visible, labels clear, camera steady — the AI will detect panel type, every component and generate the safety report.
